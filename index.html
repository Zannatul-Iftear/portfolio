Case and point:
Hardware Projects
from my academic career

1. Non-Electric Projector (NEP)

Turns out if you place a large magnifying glass at just the right distance from your phone, you can cast your display contents into a nearby wall. Thus, the NEP was born! A cardboard box with a magnifying glass and a slider to hold and adjust your smartphone.

While I can't find photos of NEP in its working state, the photos showing that we got first prize in the Science Fair should suffice!

2. Offline Ambulance Tracking System

Imagine the Uber app, but for calling Ambulances. Now imagine the app working without internet! That is exactly what I developed with my peers as a university project. The system uses SMS to communicate with an ambulance, which sends its GPS coordinateds to be read by our custom built Android application and shown on a map.

See this project in all its glory on our project website →

3. Maze Solving Robot

As part of an Electronics course in university, my friends and I built a maze solving robot that uses three sound emitters and sonar sensors to detect walls, and finds its way out of any maze using the algorithm we coded into the Arduino Uno.

I've learnt a lot about mechanics, Arduino coding and troubleshooting through this project. It taught me how to think like an engineer!
